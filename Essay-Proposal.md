## Working Title

**"Manufacturing the Quantum City: Platform-Mediated State Entrepreneurialism and Aesthetic Exclusion on Shanghai's Fuxing Island"**

(中文工作标题:《打造"量子城市":上海复兴岛的平台中介型国家企业主义与审美排斥》)

---

## One-paragraph summary (for the email to Johanna)

> My essay examines Fuxing Island (复兴岛), a small former shipyard island in Shanghai's Huangpu River that was kept as an undeveloped "strategic reserve" for roughly 15 years before being rebranded in December 2024 as a state-backed "Quantum City" innovation hub, and further activated in August 2025 through RED LAND — an immersive ACGN (anime/comics/games) event hosted in partnership with the social media platform Xiaohongshu. Drawing on the Gentrification unit's theoretical tools (Smith's rent gap, Davidson's new-build/state-led gentrification, Lees's critique of social-mix discourse), I analyze how a state-owned development consortium used a specific combination of symbolic language, landmark objects, and staged events (following Yanow's framework, as applied by Savini & Dembski to Amsterdam-Noord) to convert an idle industrial asset into a monetizable cultural brand — and how consumers' own social-media "check-in" behaviour (a form of what Cao terms "prosumption") became part of the mechanism, generating "digital capital" that the state-linked developer then converted into trackable commercial revenue via a voucher scheme tied to 500+ local merchants. Rather than framing this through classic displacement (which the evidence on Fuxing Island does not clearly support, given its small pre-existing population), I argue the case illustrates a softer, platform-era exclusion mechanism — aesthetic distinction and access thresholds rather than price-driven eviction — and I compare this to Harvey's account of 1980s Baltimore and to Yrjänä's (2026) account of anti-segregation discourse legitimizing state-led gentrification in Helsinki, to situate the Chinese case within, and against, existing gentrification theory.

---

## Core research question

**How does a state-led "asset activation" of vacant urban land — mediated through social media platforms and staged cultural events — produce a form of exclusion that does not rely on the classic mechanism of price-driven displacement, and what does this reveal about the limits of Western gentrification theory when applied to platform-era, state-entrepreneurial urban development in China?**

## Thesis statement

Fuxing Island's transformation demonstrates a **platform-mediated form of state entrepreneurialism**: the state (via a joint-venture development company) manufactures a place-brand through a coordinated sequence of symbolic language, landmark objects, and staged events, while relying on visitors' own social-media "prosumption" to generate the digital visibility that gets converted into commercial revenue. Because the island had little pre-existing residential population, this process does not produce classic gentrification-style displacement — but it does produce **exclusion through aesthetic distinction and eligibility gatekeeping** (who gets access to subsidized "talent" housing vs. who merely visits for a weekend event; whose daily use of the waterfront is curtailed by event-period access controls), a mechanism closer to Cao's (2025) "gentrification aesthetics" than to Marcuse's classic displacement.

## Key theoretical framework (beyond course readings, from the 3+ new sources)

1. **Harvey (1989)** — urban entrepreneurialism, esp. strategy of competing via the "spatial division of consumption" (tourism, spectacle, cultural innovation) — theoretical starting point
2. **Wu (2018) / Feng, Zhang & Wu (2026)** — "state entrepreneurialism": state retains central decision-making but mobilizes market instruments (here: a state-owned joint venture, 6:4 capital ratio) rather than ceding control to private developers
3. **Savini & Dembski (2016)** — Yanow's language/objects/acts framework for how symbolic politics manufacture a "creative city," applied to Amsterdam-Noord; directly transposable to Fuxing Island's "Quantum City" branding
4. **Cao (Urban Geography, and IJURR 2025)** — "prosumption" and "gentrification aesthetics" / exclusion via aesthetic distinction rather than price — the critical framework best matched to Fuxing Island's actual evidence base
5. **Yrjänä (2026)** — comparative case: Helsinki's "anti-segregation" discourse legitimizing state-led gentrification, structurally parallel to Fuxing Island's "People's City" discourse

## Evidence base (primary/grey literature)

- Official Shanghai/Yangpu government announcements: strategic-reserve status (2009–2024), "Quantum City" launch (Dec 2024), RED LAND event (Aug 2025), "Fuxing Island Music Festival" and voucher-redemption commercial scheme (May 2026)
- Government transportation-control notice + Dinghai Road Sub-district "island affairs council" (岛事会) minutes — evidence of residual users' (delivery workers, water-utility workers, small number of residents) access being converted into a permit system during the event
- Feng, Zhang & Wu (2026) and den Hartog (2021) — two peer-reviewed academic sources whose field research independently covers this exact site/district and corroborate the governance structure and (in den Hartog's case) worker exclusion from the waterfront due to "time poverty"
